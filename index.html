What We Do

Popular Research Areas

Our department spans the most sought-after disciplines in modern chemistry. Whether your passion lies in synthesis, computation, materials, or biology — there's a lab for you.

Organic & Synthetic Chemistry

Total synthesis, method development, catalysis, C–H functionalization, and transition-metal chemistry.

Bioorganic & Chemical Biology

Biocatalysis, protein engineering, peptidomimetics, and drug discovery at the chemistry–biology interface.

Biochemistry & Protein Science

Protein–protein interactions, structural biology, targeted protein degradation, and cancer therapeutics.

Computational & Theoretical Chemistry

QM/MM simulations, polarizable force fields, molecular dynamics, electronic structure theory, and machine learning.

Materials & Inorganic Chemistry

Metal-organic frameworks, porous polymers, luminescent materials, solid-state chemistry, and crystallography.

Polymer Chemistry

Semiconducting polymers, photochromic materials, dynamic covalent chemistry, and 3D-printable polymers.

Analytical & Nanochemistry

Metal nanoparticle synthesis, electrochemistry, sensors, and spectroscopic methods for biomolecular analysis.

Biophysics & Metalloprotein Science

Metal homeostasis, metalloprotein structure–function, oxidative stress, and biophysical spectroscopy.

Graduate Programs

We offer M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Chemistry with research tracks spanning every area listed above. Competitive stipends, tuition waivers, and access to state-of-the-art facilities including a national crystallography hub.

Ph.D. in Chemistry

75+ credit hours beyond B.S. Innovation-focused research with interdisciplinary electives. Qualify in Year 2.

M.S. in Chemistry

30 credit hours, full- or part-time. Thesis option with hands-on lab research. Ideal for industry or further study.
